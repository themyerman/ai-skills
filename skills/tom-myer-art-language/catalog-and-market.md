## Major series

**Indigenous Futurism** (~20 pieces)
The largest and most developed series. Indigenous figures in space: astronauts, commanders, warriors, pilots. Titles prefixed "Futurism:". Grounded in Grace Dillon's Indigenous futurism framework — the apocalypse already happened, we survived, now we go to space. Indiginaut is the defining piece.

**Political / Resist**
Encroachment, Fight Back, Resist, Make America Native Again, Fuck ICE, Destroy ICE, Red Hand, MMIW, Undefeated, Peaceful Not Harmless, Stand Your Ground. Direct, unapologetic. Collectors who find this series are committed buyers.

**Figurative women**
Deer Woman, MMIW, Ancestral Woman, Embera Woman, Beauty, Bride, Mother and Child, Joy, Grief. Indigenous women in different relationships to the world — spiritual, ceremonial, grieving, fierce. Each figure is distinctly marked.

**Animal spirits**
Coyote and Raven, Three Sister Wolves, Blood Crow, Tomahawk Crow, Medicine Wheel Crow, Grudge Crow, Dawn Eagle, Guardian Eagle, Night Hawk, Soaring Thunderbird, Wolf Singing, White Buffalo, Buffalo Dream, Three Buffalo, March of the Buffalo, Palomino, Year of the Horse. Animals carry spiritual weight and petroglyph marks inside their forms.

**Petroglyph / iconographic**
Petroglyph Raven, Thunderbird Petroglyph, Haudenosaunee Turtle with Wampum Belt, Thirteen Moon Turtle, Raven with Petroglyphs and Weapons, Warclub Tomahawk. Symbol-forward, graphic.

**Haudenosaunee-specific**
Haudenosaunee Turtle with Wampum Belt, Three Sachems, Gathering of Clans, Thirteen Moon Turtle. Cayuga/Six Nations ancestral tradition. Tom is Cayuga Nation — this work is directly autobiographical.

**Land-based / landscape**
Canyon Sunrise, Dawn River Valley, Flatirons and Buffalo Road, Glorious Morning, Afternoon Stroll, Moonlight Fishing, Sun Over Tipis, Sacred Lodge, Bless This Lodge. Land as relational — always implies presence and relationship, never pure scenery.

**Abstract**
Abstract 1–8, Fractured Native, Floating, Bullseye. Least developed commercially but formally strong. Fractured Native (cubist fragmented face) is the strongest piece in this group.

**Wildlife / birds**
Northern Goshawk, Merlin, Superb Owl, Curious Cubs, Friends, Walk in the Woods. More naturalistic, less iconographic than the animal spirits series.

**What the bestsellers reveal:**
- **Animal spirits dominate** (Blood Crow, Dawn Eagle, Night Hawk, Soaring Thunderbird, Petroglyph Raven, Year of the Horse) — the single strongest commercial category
- **Political work sells** (Encroachment, Make America Native Again) — these buyers are committed, not casual
- **Haudenosaunee-specific work sells** (Haudenosaunee Turtle with Wampum Belt) — specificity is not a liability
- **Land-based narrative sells** (Flatirons and Buffalo Road, Moonlight Fishing, Ride Hard)
- **Emotional resonance sells** (Grief)
- **No abstract pieces in the bestseller list** — abstracts are formally interesting but commercially weakest

## New work in progress (as of May 2026)

Decisions from first comprehensive catalog review:

**New series — Homeland**
Reworked landscapes with Indigenous life woven in: longhouses, tipis, canoes. Land as inhabited home, not scenery. Pre-contact flourishing — quiet, ordinary, assertive. 3–6 pieces.

**New series — Warpaint**
3–4 abstract color field pieces. Horizontal color bands, two dark eye voids, vague hint of face. Celebrates warpaint as ceremony without attributing specific colors to specific nations. Reclaims the word on Indigenous terms.

**Reworks from early catalog:**
- **Petroglyph Turtle** → Full rebuild as Turtle Island / Haudenosaunee creation story. Hand/spiral symbol stays as anchor. Highest priority.
- **Mohawk** → Abstract the face further (Fractured Native territory). Vertical stripes radiate beyond the face into the background. Keep eyes and gold accent as anchors.
- **Sun Worship** → Clothed figure, back to viewer, facing sun. Visible skin is all tattoos — petroglyph marks, clan symbols, handprints.
- **Free to Roam** → Three wolves (Wolf Clan piece). Drop zig-zag ray. Add subtle inner marks to wolf silhouettes.

## Cayuga Language Series (2024) — NOT YET ON SITE

Five digital pieces incorporating spoken Cayuga language via speech bubbles. Created in 2024, never added to myerman.art. **Next immediate task: add these to the site.**

The speech bubble device makes language alive and present-tense — figures speaking Cayuga to the viewer or to each other, not text as label or exhibit. This is the most culturally significant work in the catalog and directly addresses language preservation. Also the work most likely to attract institutional attention.

**The five pieces:**
1. Portrait with teal mandala background — woman's face, facial tattoos, dense background, speech bubble with Cayuga text
2. Two figures facing each other across a large golden sun — most formally beautiful piece in the series; intimate, tattoo marks visible
3. Standing contact-era warrior — historically accurate trade cloth coat + traditional accessories + musket; speech bubble in Cayuga
4. Young warrior with feather roach — bold graphic figure, torn-paper speech bubble, teal sky and sun
5. Mohawk profile — cross-hatched background, yellow sun, speech bubble in Cayuga; companion piece to the Mohawk rework
